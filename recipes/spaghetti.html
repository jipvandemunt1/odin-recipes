Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti is a classic Italian pasta dish loved for its simplicity and versatility. Made from long, thin strands of pasta, it pairs perfectly with a variety of sauces, from rich and meaty Bolognese to light and fresh tomato or garlic-based sauces. Traditionally, spaghetti is cooked in boiling salted water until al dente, ensuring a firm yet tender texture that absorbs flavors well.

Once drained, the pasta is often tossed with a savory sauce, complemented by ingredients like olive oil, garlic, basil, or Parmesan cheese. Whether served with a hearty meat sauce, creamy carbonara, or just a drizzle of olive oil and herbs, spaghetti remains a comforting, satisfying meal enjoyed by people of all ages.

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 250g (8 oz) spaghetti
  • Water (for boiling)
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (400g / 14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Serving

  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for spice)

Steps

  1. Boil the pasta
  2. Prepare the sauce
  3. Combine the pasta and sauce
  4. Serve and enjoy
